EU Summer Social Strategy

Summer of Selective Presence

A framework for operating exclusivity as both brand signal and growth engine across five European markets, anchored by controlled access, earned moments, and intentional cultural presence.

Part 1

The Summer Narrative Arc

The EU summer lives in three distinct movements. Each with its own social gravity. By August, membership is defined not by what Dorsia offers, but by what members have already experienced that culture at large only knows about retroactively.

Milan: Year-Round Thesis

Milan is structurally different. It's a proof-of-concept for year-round exclusivity mechanics. The member base is entirely non-overlapping with Mediterranean markets. No seasonal travel coincidence.

This means Milan's social strategy operates on a different rhythm: founder and creative-led credibility building, not moment-chasing. Early launches feature chefs with established reputation in Italian food media, designers who've defined the Milan restaurant aesthetic.

By August, Milan has its own community inside the Discord, its own handful of early launches, and a foundation for year-round social that doesn't depend on seasonal moments. The insight members get: Dorsia doesn't need summer to be relevant. It's culturally grounded everywhere.

Part 2

Exclusivity as Conversion Engine

One experiment designed to answer: Do exclusive access mechanics build brand affinity faster than public content, and does that affinity convert to bookings?

The Hypothesis

Members who receive exclusive 48-hour pre-public access to new partner restaurant launches will demonstrate measurably higher booking intent, stronger sentiment alignment with brand values, and more frequent engagement than members exposed only to public content.

Specific targets (calibrated week 1): 1.8–2.2x higher booking rate, 35–40% higher sentiment strength, 1.3–1.5x engagement lift.

What We Build & Test

Three Parallel Tests

DM-only Stories access, Discord community, and public Instagram launch as control group.

This creates a measurement framework that isolates the impact of exclusivity from other variables.

DM-Only Stories

3–5 story sequence sent 48 hours before public. Functional previews: space, details, plate, team moment, timing.

Milan Discord

Private community for 200 early members. Channels for announcements, dining discussion, cultural moments, exclusive RSVPs.

Public Launch

Standard Instagram carousel post launching 48 hours after DM access. Direct comparison cohort.

8-Week Experiment Window: June 1 – July 31

Week 1: Setup
Discord launches. First cohort invited. Creative briefs finalized. Sentiment classifier training begins.
Week 2-3: Launch Phase
2–3 venue launches through full protocol. All tracking activated. Early engagement analyzed daily.
Week 4-6: Refinement Phase
2–3 more venues. Analyze Cohort 1–2 data. Adjust creative if needed. Discord community building accelerates.
Week 7-8: Final Analysis
Last 2–3 launches. Consolidate booking data. Sentiment analysis reaches critical mass. Begin findings prep.

Decision Framework

Scenario 1: Exclusive Access Drives Both Booking Lift & Sentiment Strength

The data: Exclusive access group has 1.8x–2x higher booking rate, positive sentiment is 35–40% stronger, Discord participation sustains at 10–15% active weekly.

The insight: Exclusivity is both a brand asset AND a conversion tool. Members want to feel like insiders, and that feeling translates to action.

Next year: Scale this model. Every launch is tiered (members first → limited public → full public). Expand Discord to all markets. Make exclusivity the operational playbook for all social-to-booking pathways.

Part 3

Creator & Tastemaker Activation

We activate people who are simultaneously (1) already dining at our partner venues, (2) culturally trusted in their markets, and (3) likely to organically share because they're genuinely excited.

Behind-the-scenes creator access moments
Organic creator content from member dining

Who We Activate

Milan

Chefs with 2–3 restaurant groups. Design professionals. Food writers with regional authority.

  • 3–5 creators per market
  • Established reputation focus
  • Audience: 50–200K (quality over scale)

Mediterranean

Venue operators. Club promoters. People embedded in the markets all summer.

  • 2–3 per seasonal market
  • In-market authenticity
  • Direct relationship with venues

We Avoid

Travel bloggers. Macro-influencers (500K+). Anyone without demonstrated market presence.

  • No lifestyle influencers
  • No paid partnerships
  • No "influencer" language

Activation & Measurement

No paid partnerships. No gifted access announcements. We treat them as early members. They get exclusive access. If they share organically, great. If not, no loss—they're part of the ecosystem.

Success Metrics

We measure organic seeding, audience impact, and conversation lift. A single creator driving 1–3 qualified signups and modest brand awareness over 8 weeks is a win.

Organic Seeding Rate

35–45% of activated creators share Dorsia moments without being asked. Measured across Stories, posts, captions.

Audience Impact

Search volume for "Dorsia" after creator posts. Waitlist signups correlated with mentions.

Conversation Lift

Post-season survey: how did member-converts hear about Dorsia? Segment by creator mentions vs. other channels.

Next Year's Playbook

  1. Expand the exclusivity tier system. Every launch is tiered: members-only → limited public → full public. This is our operating model.
  2. Scale Discord to all markets. Replicate Milan Discord in Mykonos, Ibiza, Monaco, St. Tropez. Use for high-value moments.
  3. Refine creator activation. Fewer creators, deeper relationships. Focus on chefs and restaurateurs with followings <100K.
  4. Integrate cultural calendar as social engine. Members' calendar events automatically surface in strategy.
  5. Year-round Milan strategy. Develop content verticals that don't depend on seasonal moments: founder stories, design deep-dives, Italian cuisine seasons.

By next summer, a member should expect that Dorsia social shows them moments before culture at large discovers them. That's the signal we've proven works.
