Milan: Year-Round Thesis

Milan is structurally different. It's a proof-of-concept for year-round exclusivity mechanics. The member base is entirely non-overlapping with Mediterranean markets. No seasonal travel coincidence.

This means Milan's social strategy operates on a different rhythm: founder and creative-led credibility building, not moment-chasing. Early launches feature chefs with established reputation in Italian food media, designers who've defined the Milan restaurant aesthetic.

By August, Milan has its own community inside the Discord, its own handful of early launches, and a foundation for year-round social that doesn't depend on seasonal moments. The insight members get: Dorsia doesn't need summer to be relevant. It's culturally grounded everywhere.

Platform Strategy

For this audience and this season, platform choice is a brand decision. Where Dorsia shows up — and where it refuses to — is part of the product.

Instagram — Primary

The only platform where Dorsia's aesthetic, exclusivity mechanics, and member behavior all converge. Stories for the inner circle. Feed for cultural authority. DMs as the highest-trust channel we own.

  • Feed: Consistent editorial cadence
  • Stories: 48h member-exclusive access windows
  • DMs: The most exclusive channel — used sparingly and with intent

TikTok — Not the Right Fit Yet

TikTok rewards discovery at scale. For a brand where access is limited by design, broad organic reach works against the experience. This season, the focus stays on depth over distribution — revisit as the member base and brand positioning mature.

  • Deprioritized this season in favor of higher-trust channels
  • Revisit when member data signals meaningful platform usage
  • A considered choice, open to evolving

Quality Over Quantity

Growth is measured by who engages, not how many. Follower count, reach, and impressions say very little about whether the right people are paying attention. The goal is a smaller, precise audience that genuinely reflects the Dorsia member profile.

  • Organic growth earned through content, not tactics
  • Engagement quality prioritized over post frequency
  • The right 50 accounts matter more than 5,000 indifferent ones

The Partner Launch Lifecycle

Social's role is different at each stage of a restaurant launch. The mistake is treating launch day as the moment. It's not — it's the middle of the story.

Before / During / After

Before — Build Ambient Tension
No announcement. No reveal. Members receive access 48 hours before public — via Close Friends Stories (the green ring does the work visually) or direct DMs for the highest-tier moments. Atmospheric content only: space, light, a detail. The public doesn't know something is coming. Members do. That gap is the value.
During — Editorial, Not Promotional
Launch content is documentary, not marketing. Real moments from the first service. Chef, table, plate, room. No "now open" language. No booking CTA. The content makes you want to have been there — not tells you to go.
After — Amplify the Room
The people who were there become the story. UGC from early members gets amplified. Creator content goes up. The restaurant earns its place in the Dorsia canon not through promotion but through proof of the moment.
The Rule
Social never shows open tables, availability, or inventory. If a post could be read as "we have room," it's the wrong post. Desire is built by making people feel like they might not get in — not by reassuring them that they can.

The Cultural Calendar as Signal

Dorsia maintains a live cultural calendar in-app. The mistake is treating it as a content queue — posting "Cannes Film Festival starts today" is noise. The insight is using it as intelligence.

What the calendar tells us: Where our members will be, and when. Social's job is to be there first — not to narrate what's happening, but to surface the specific restaurant, table, or moment that matters within it.

The execution: When the calendar shows a cluster of members orienting around the Monaco Grand Prix, we don't post about F1. We post the dinner that the people who matter are having the night before the race. The calendar is a map. Social is what you find when you zoom in.

What we never do: Turn the cultural calendar into a feed of app notifications. "Did you know Cannes is in our app?" is the death of brand credibility. The calendar is intelligence. Social is the expression of it.

What Social Should Not Do

Never Drive Bookings Directly

No "Reserve now." No "Limited tables." No link in bio pointing to availability. Social builds desire. The app converts it. The moment social starts functioning as a booking channel, it stops being a brand asset.

Never Chase Reach

A post that reaches 200,000 people and gets seen by 180,000 wrong ones has done damage. Dorsia's social strategy accepts a smaller, slower audience in exchange for a precisely right one. We do not optimize for scale.

Never Explain Itself

Social should never caption-explain what Dorsia is, how it works, or why it's worth joining. If a post requires context to land, it's the wrong post. The brand should be legible from the aesthetic alone.

Part 2 — Experimentation

One question. Eight weeks. A clear answer.

Part 2

Exclusivity as Conversion Engine

One experiment designed to answer: Do exclusive access mechanics build brand affinity faster than public content, and does that affinity convert to bookings?

The Hypothesis

Members who receive exclusive 48-hour pre-public access to new partner restaurant launches will demonstrate measurably higher booking intent, stronger sentiment alignment with brand values, and more frequent engagement than members exposed only to public content.

Success looks like meaningful lift in booking intent, stronger sentiment alignment, and higher engagement frequency among the exclusive access cohort versus the control group.

What We Build & Test

Three Parallel Tests

Close Friends Stories and DM access for members, Discord community for Milan, and public Instagram launch as control group.

This creates a measurement framework that isolates the impact of exclusivity from other variables.

Close Friends + DM Access

3–5 story sequence via Close Friends list, 48 hours before public. The green ring signals exclusivity before anyone even taps. DMs reserved for the highest-tier moments — when the access itself is the message.

Milan Discord

A curated founding cohort of early members. Channels for announcements, dining discussion, cultural moments, exclusive RSVPs.

Public Launch

Standard Instagram carousel post launching 48 hours after DM access. Direct comparison cohort.

8-Week Experiment Window: June 1 – July 31

Week 1: Setup
Discord launches. First cohort invited. Creative briefs finalized. Sentiment classifier training begins.
Week 2-3: Launch Phase
2–3 venue launches through full protocol. All tracking activated. Early engagement analyzed daily.
Week 4-6: Refinement Phase
2–3 more venues. Analyze Cohort 1–2 data. Adjust creative if needed. Discord community building accelerates.
Week 7-8: Final Analysis
Last 2–3 launches. Consolidate booking data. Sentiment analysis reaches critical mass. Begin findings prep.

Decision Framework

Scenario 1: Exclusive Access Drives Both Booking Lift & Sentiment Strength

The data: Exclusive access group shows measurably higher booking intent, stronger positive sentiment, and sustained Discord participation week over week.

The insight: Exclusivity is both a brand asset AND a conversion tool. Members want to feel like insiders, and that feeling translates to action.

Next year: Scale this model. Every launch is tiered (members first → limited public → full public). Expand Discord to all markets. Make exclusivity the operational playbook for all social-to-booking pathways.

Conversation Lift

How We Find Them In-Market

We don't build creator lists in advance. We monitor in real time and move fast.

In-Market Identification Process

Step 1 — Geotag Intelligence
Monitor Instagram geotags for Dorsia partner venues in each market continuously through May. Who's posting from Mykonos's top restaurants before the season peaks? Those are the people already embedded in the market — not tourists passing through.
Step 2 — Member Cross-Reference
Cross-reference identified creators against the Dorsia member list. Existing members already have a genuine relationship with the product — the conversation starts there. For non-members, warm outreach flows through partner introductions, never a cold DM.
Step 3 — Activation
No formal brief. No gifted access announcement. We extend early member access for the season and let behavior follow. If they share, we amplify. We never ask them to post, tag, or disclose. The moment it feels like a partnership, the authenticity is gone.
Step 4 — In-Market Coordinator
One person per city with relationships at partner venues. Their job: make sure the right creators get the right table at the right moment. They don't post. They facilitate. The content finds its own way out.

What We're Measuring

Primary Signal

Booking Intent

Sentiment Strength

Depth Signal

The Honest Retrospective

What Worked

The preview economy. Members trained themselves to check Close Friends and DMs first within three weeks. The exclusivity window became a behavioral habit — members built it into how they use the product. That's the proof the mechanic has legs beyond this summer.

What Didn't

Milan Discord growth was slower than projected without an IRL anchor moment at launch. A community built purely online, in a city built around physical presence and in-person credibility, needed a real-world event to spark it. We skipped that step. We shouldn't have.

What Changes

Every new market Discord launches with a physical event — a dinner, a tasting, anything that puts the right 30 people in a room first. The digital community follows the IRL one. And we hire one in-market person per city rather than running everything remotely.

Measuring Brand Equity — Not Performance

Engagement rate tells you what happened. Brand equity tells you what it meant. They are different questions requiring different methods.

The core problem: Standard social metrics (reach, impressions, engagement rate) measure distribution, not desire. A brand that is everywhere and means nothing scores well on performance metrics. Dorsia should score poorly on reach and perfectly on resonance.

Editorial Mention Tracking

How often is Dorsia referenced unprompted by luxury, food, and culture media? Not press releases — organic citations. A single mention in a Condé Nast piece carries more brand equity signal than 50,000 impressions on a reel.

Language Analysis

What words do members use when describing Dorsia to someone who's never heard of it? We survey members at season end. If the language clusters around "exclusive," "ahead of the curve," "not for everyone" — the brand positioning is working. If it clusters around "useful" — it isn't.

Follower Quality Audit

Who followed us this summer? Not how many. Are they the right accounts — restaurant owners, cultural figures, relevant media, aspirational members? 200 follows from the right people is a brand equity win. 2,000 follows from the wrong audience is a warning sign.

Next Year's Playbook

  1. Expand the exclusivity tier system. Every launch is tiered: members-only → limited public → full public. This is our operating model.
  2. Scale Discord to all markets. Replicate Milan Discord in Mykonos, Ibiza, Monaco, St. Tropez. Use for high-value moments.
  3. Refine creator activation. Fewer creators, deeper relationships. Focus on chefs and restaurateurs with followings <100K.
  4. Integrate cultural calendar as social engine. Members' calendar events automatically surface in strategy.
  5. Year-round Milan strategy. Develop content verticals that don't depend on seasonal moments: founder stories, design deep-dives, Italian cuisine seasons.

By next summer, a member should expect that Dorsia social shows them moments before culture at large discovers them. That's the signal we've proven works.
